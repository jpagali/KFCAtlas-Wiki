=== BYTE COMMERCE BACKEND: Overview - Commerce Backend ===

Impact Team Byte-Helium Functional Requirement/Overview - Commerce BackendUpdated Nov 18, 2025EditShareCopy linkMore actionsOverview - Commerce BackendBy Wesley.weidemann8 min6Add a reaction1. Product descriptionByte Commerce Backend is the operational engine and API layer of the Byte Commerce platform.From the combined architecture and requirements documents it:Powers omnichannel ordering and fulfilment across web, app, kiosk, third-party aggregators, POS and delivery partners.Centrally manages menus, prices, promotions and content, with support for localisation, tax rules and audit history.Handles secure payments and refunds via multiple payment providers and local methods.Manages digital customer data, including migration of existing accounts into the Byte customer domain and persistent carts with abandonment / expiry handling.Exposes composable APIs for front-ends (eg Helium, native apps, kiosks) and for integration platforms such as Byte Connect.We treat "Byte Commerce Backend" as a product-level component within the overall Byte platform, distinct from but tightly coupled to:Byte Commerce Front-end (Helium)Byte Portal (admin + operational tooling)Byte Menu, Byte POS and KDS / kitchen and fleet capabilities.2. PurposeHigh-level purposes of Byte Commerce Backend:Provide a single, stable API surface for all commerce channels (web, app, kiosk, aggregators) to create and manage orders.Enforce brand and market rules for menus, pricing, tax, promotions and eligibility.Persist and orchestrate shopping carts, orders, payments and fulfilment signals between channels, POS and KDS / delivery.Maintain a trusted source of customer data for authentication, profiles and migration from legacy systems.Support safe evolution of capabilities via versioned APIs, data contracts and configuration, without disrupting channels or markets.In documentation terms, this Overview page exists to:Explain what Byte Commerce Backend is and is not.Show how the FRDs, TRDs and Data Contract registries fit together.Give different stakeholders a clear entry point into the documentation set.3. === BYTE PORTAL: Portal - Overview and Glossary ===

Portal acts as an internal operational and configuration surface for capabilities powered by Byte Commerce and related Byte services, rather than a customer-facing ordering channel.

Key Roles:
- Business Role: Operational and configuration surface for Store Manager and operations
- Operational Role: Hosts Store Manager functionality for store-level employees, area coaches, and franchise owners
- Data Role: Stores restaurant data (e.g. restaurant attributes) and configuration that feeds customer journeys where Byte Commerce provides store location and promotion data
- Technology Role: Web front-end application that consumes Byte Commerce APIs, aligned with Helium and native app products

Artifacts:
- FRD (Functional Requirements Documents): FRD-PORT-00x format, describing Portal capabilities and features
- TRD (Technical Reference Documents): TRD-PORT-[Domain] format, standardizing Portal integrations with Byte Commerce, SSO, permissions, and other services
- FRD-TRD Master Index: Portal – FRD-TRD Master Index showing how each FRD maps to relevant TRD domains
- TRD Domain Catalogue: Portal – TRD Domain Catalogue listing technical domains
- Data Contracts & Interfaces: Byte Portal – Data Contracts & Interfaces registry of all canonical data contracts and APIs
