Recommended feature direction

BUILD A WHAT'S NEW CENTER NOT JUST RELEASE NOTES

The recommended product pattern is an in-product release awareness layer with role-based targeting. Each release item should answer four questions fast: what changed, why the business should care, how to use it, and when this is relevant. This turns “we never know what shipped” into a habit loop users can trust without forcing them to go hunt in documentation.

Core recommendation

Use a “What’s New Center” with targeted badges, dismissible highlight cards, and a searchable archive. Keep release notes as the long-tail record, but promote a shorter, user-centered summary in-product.

PRD

Problem statement

Market users do not reliably know what features were released recently, which leads to low awareness, repeated enablement work, slower adoption, and the perception that the platform is not improving even when meaningful functionality ships.

  • Release notes are too passive and require users to seek them out.
  • Users need business relevance, not only a changelog.
  • Different roles care about different launches.
  • Teams need a faster path from awareness to first use.

PRD

Goals and non-goals

  • Increase awareness of relevant new features among market users.
  • Improve time-to-understanding for newly shipped functionality.
  • Drive first-use and adoption of targeted launches.
  • Reduce reliance on manual stakeholder reminders.
  • Non-goal: replace full documentation or training programs.
  • Non-goal: create a feed for every tiny backend change.

PRD

Target users

  • Market operators who need to know what is newly available and whether it affects their workflows.
  • Regional managers who need business framing and rollout clarity.
  • Admin users who need quick enablement for newly shipped tasks or settings.

PRD

Required content model

  • What’s new: concise feature headline and release status.
  • Why it matters: business problem solved and expected value.
  • How to use it: short path to first action or guide.
  • Where it fits: example business cases and relevant roles/markets.

PRD

Success metrics

  • Open rate on “What’s New” items by relevant audience.
  • Click-through rate from announcement to guide or workflow.
  • Feature adoption among targeted users within 30 days.
  • Reduction in “I didn’t know this exists” feedback.

PRD

Scope v1

  • One inbox-style “What’s New” center in the portal.
  • One homepage highlight card for recent important launches.
  • Per-item structure with summary, value, usage, and examples.
  • Dismiss state and “mark as read” only.
  • No comments, no reactions, no complex personalization engine in v1.
